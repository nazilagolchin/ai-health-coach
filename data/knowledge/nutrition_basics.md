# Nutrition Basics for Everyday Wellness

## The Foundation of Nourishing Eating

Wellness nutrition is not about perfection or restriction — it's about consistency and nourishment. The goal is to eat in a way that gives your body steady energy, supports your mood, strengthens your immune system, and helps you feel well day to day.

## Macronutrients: What Your Body Needs Every Day

**Carbohydrates** are your body's preferred fuel source. Choose complex carbs — oats, sweet potatoes, brown rice, quinoa, legumes, fruits, and vegetables — over refined carbs like white bread or sugary snacks. Complex carbs release energy slowly and keep blood sugar stable.

**Proteins** are the building blocks for muscle repair, hormone production, and immune function. Good sources include eggs, fish, chicken, tofu, lentils, chickpeas, Greek yogurt, and nuts. Aim to include a protein source at every meal.

**Healthy Fats** support brain function, hormone balance, and cell repair. Avocado, olive oil, nuts, seeds, and oily fish (salmon, sardines, mackerel) are excellent choices. Fat is not the enemy — poor quality fat is.

## The Power of Micronutrients

Vitamins and minerals work behind the scenes to keep everything running. Eating a colorful variety of vegetables and fruits each day is the easiest way to cover your bases. "Eat the rainbow" is simple but effective advice.

Key nutrients to pay attention to:
- **Magnesium** (dark leafy greens, pumpkin seeds, dark chocolate): supports sleep, muscle relaxation, and mood
- **Vitamin D** (sunlight, fatty fish, fortified foods): essential for mood, immunity, and bone health
- **Iron** (lentils, spinach, red meat, pumpkin seeds): vital for energy; low iron causes fatigue
- **B vitamins** (whole grains, eggs, leafy greens): critical for energy metabolism and nervous system health
- **Omega-3 fatty acids** (flaxseed, walnuts, oily fish): anti-inflammatory, support brain and heart health

## Hydration

Water is the most underrated wellness tool. Even mild dehydration causes fatigue, brain fog, and increased stress responses. Aim for 6–8 glasses of water per day, more if active or in hot weather.

Herbal teas count toward hydration. Coffee in moderate amounts is fine. Sugary drinks and excess alcohol are dehydrating and pro-inflammatory.

## Practical Eating Habits That Actually Work

1. **Eat breakfast** — breaking the overnight fast with protein and fiber stabilizes blood sugar and energy for the day
2. **Don't skip meals** — regular meals prevent energy crashes and reduce cravings
3. **Slow down** — it takes 20 minutes for satiety signals to reach the brain; eating slowly prevents overeating
4. **Plate balance** — half plate vegetables, quarter protein, quarter complex carbs is a simple visual guide
5. **Meal prep basics** — cooking grains and proteins in batches reduces daily decision fatigue
6. **Limit ultra-processed foods** — not because they're morally wrong, but because they lack the micronutrients your body needs and drive inflammation

## Mindful Eating

Mindful eating means paying attention to hunger and fullness cues, eating without distraction when possible, and noticing how different foods make you feel afterward. It is one of the most powerful shifts for sustainable healthy eating and helps develop a positive relationship with food.

## What Wellness Nutrition Is NOT

This is general wellness guidance for healthy adults. It is not a medical diet plan.
Anyone with diabetes, kidney disease, heart disease, eating disorders, or other medical conditions should work with a registered dietitian for personalized dietary guidance.
